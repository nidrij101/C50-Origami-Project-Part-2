Intresting Facts aobut Camels

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Intresting Facts aobut Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Origami Teddy Bear!!

Intresting Facts aobut Teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  • Washable teddy bears didn't appear until 1954 when the stuffing was switched to an easier to clean nylon fill. Before that, they were stuffed with mohair, wool, and other hard to clean fills.

Origami Panda!!

Intresting Facts aobut Panda

  • The giant panda's distinct black-and-white markings have two functions: camouflage and communication.
  • Like domestic cats, giant pandas have vertical slits for pupils
  • At 5 months old, giant pandas learn how to climb - sometimes practicing by climbing on their mum.

Origami Flying Cicada!!

Intresting Facts aobut Flying Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus. (Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings.) Cicadas don’t sing at night, so don’t blame them if you can’t sleep.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
